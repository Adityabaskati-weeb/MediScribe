# MediScribe Hackathon Readiness Scorecard

Last updated: 2026-05-17

This scorecard rates MediScribe against the current Gemma 4 Good Hackathon
judging shape: impact and vision, video/storytelling, and technical depth and
execution.

## Overall Read

MediScribe is now a serious track-prize contender. The product story is strong,
the codebase is real, the offline use case is compelling, and the Unsloth model
artifact is public. The biggest remaining gap is not engineering depth. It is
submission packaging: public demo quality, video sharpness, and making the first
30 seconds unforgettable.

## What Still Blocks A Top Finish

1. The public video is still the highest-risk piece.
2. The live demo must be frictionless and judge-safe.
3. The first 20-30 seconds need one unforgettable clinic moment.
4. The visual polish should feel intentional, not prototype-like.
5. The writeup must stay honest while still sounding bold.

## Highest-Leverage Final Moves

1. Record the maternal emergency flow in airplane mode.
2. Show the stroke flow as the second hero case.
3. Put the public adapter and job link directly in the writeup.
4. Make the dashboard screenshot look operational, not decorative.
5. Tighten the video around one sentence: "Sync can wait. Red flags cannot."
