# AI Dubbing Handoff Checklist

Use this after the English master video is locked.

Reference tool: `https://aidubbing.io/`

## What The Tool Needs

Prepare these before upload:

- Final dubbed input video in `MP4`
- Runtime under `10 minutes`
- File size under `60 MB`
- Clean English narration already mixed
- Final script locked before upload
- Medical terms checked in advance

## Best Workflow

1. Finish the English master edit first.
2. Export a compressed dubbing copy.
3. Upload to `aidubbing.io`.
4. Choose the target language.
5. Choose a calm, trustworthy voice.
6. Review transcription carefully.
7. Fix medical terms before generating the dubbed version.
8. Preview the full dubbed video before download.

## Recommended Export For Upload

For the dubbing upload copy:

- Format: `MP4`
- Resolution: `720p`
- Video codec: `H.264`
- Audio codec: `AAC`
- Audio bitrate: `96-128 kbps`
- Target size: below `60 MB`

Keep a separate full-quality master for YouTube.

## Medical Terms To Review Manually

Double-check these in the transcript step:

- MediScribe
- Gemma
- Ollama
- referral
- preeclampsia
- postpartum hemorrhage
- stroke
- sepsis
- oxygen saturation
- rural clinic

## Voice Selection Guidance

Pick a voice that feels:

- calm
- clear
- trustworthy
- medically serious

Avoid:

- playful voices
- exaggerated emotions
- overly theatrical voices
- voices that feel like social media ads

## Subtitle Guidance

If you add subtitles:

- keep them concise
- avoid dense multi-line captions
- make sure medical terms match the app screens
- prefer sentence casing over all caps

## Final Dubbing QA

Before you publish the dubbed version, check:

- pronunciation of medical terms
- pronunciation of `MediScribe`
- timing against on-screen actions
- subtitle accuracy
- no mistranslated emergency words like `refer now`, `urgent`, `stroke`, `bleeding`

## Deliverables To Keep

Save all three:

1. English master video
2. Compressed dubbing upload copy
3. Final dubbed export
